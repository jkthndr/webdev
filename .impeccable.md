# Design Context

## Target Audience

Oliver is the primary reader. He is technical, product-minded, and comfortable making decisions from structured information, but long walls of text are hard to parse. Agent collaborators such as Codex, Claude, and Penny may also read these artifacts.

## Use Cases

- Understand research findings quickly.
- Compare tools, architectures, and product options.
- Decide what to build first.
- Track MVP cut lines, risks, and next technical moves.
- Share a plan with other agents without forcing everyone through a long markdown document.

## Brand Personality And Tone

The visual docs should feel warm, sharp, and practical. The reference mood is an audit or launch guide: coral, warm orange, gold, sage, cream paper, clear hierarchy, cards, tabs, stat blocks, and timelines.

The tone should be plainspoken and decisive:

- Bottom line first.
- Short blocks instead of dense paragraphs.
- Practical labels like `Must have`, `Defer`, `Do not build`, `Risk`, and `Next move`.
- Calm but not sterile.

## Visual Documentation Preferences

- Prefer single-file static HTML in `docs/` for long human-readable planning docs.
- Use cards, tabs, comparison tables, timelines, callouts, and compact source-link sections.
- Keep markdown as the durable source when useful, but pair it with a visual companion for review.
- Avoid generic landing-page composition. These are working documents, not marketing pages.
- Avoid decorative clutter that does not help scanning or decision-making.
