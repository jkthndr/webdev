# Landing page layout patterns

A short menu of patterns that work for V1. Pick one based on the brief, do not chain all of them.

## Hero / Features / CTA

The default. Use when the brief gives a clear product statement, a few real capabilities, and one obvious next step.

Section order: header, hero with dual CTA, three feature blocks, final CTA, footer.

When to skip pricing: free product, internal tool surface, or pricing not yet decided. Replace with FAQ or a longer-form value section.

## Hero / Features / Social proof / Pricing / CTA

Use when the brief mentions real customers, real logos, or real outcomes. Drop social proof if any of those would be invented.

Section order: header, hero, features, social proof, pricing, final CTA, footer. The seed defaults to this shape and lets you remove sections.

## Hero / Long form / CTA

Use for technical or trust-heavy products where readers need more context before a CTA fires. Replace the three-column features with a longer narrative section that walks through the use case.

Section order: header, hero, long-form sections (one or two), final CTA, footer.

## Hero / Demo / Features / CTA

Use when there is a real interactive demo, video, or screenshot worth leading with. Put the demo above the features. Do not fake a demo.

## What never works

- Hero plus six feature columns plus stat row plus testimonials plus FAQ plus pricing plus CTA plus newsletter plus footer. Cut.
- Two heroes (a small one above the main one). Pick one.
- Sticky promotional banner on a page that is itself trying to convert. Pick one job per surface.
- Side-stripe cards that crowd the hero. The seed avoids this and so should generated pages.
